Enviromental Monitoring

CEMS

Depending on the kind of fuel used and local environmental regulations, a number of pollutants, preferably CO, NOx and SO2 are to be monitored continuously in the flue gas at the stack along with dust (particulate matter, PM), gas flow, and the reference values H2O, temperature and O2. The monitored data is transferred to a specific data acquisition system for further processing and reporting to the authorities. In case of co-incineration of alternative fuels, additional components such as HCl, HF, Hg and VOC may be required to be monitored as well

AQMS

The most common form of air quality monitoring is ambient air monitoring. Ambient air monitoring involves the measurement of “representative” air in urban, industrial and rural areas. These measurements are used to determine the human exposure to pollutants and can be combined with trace/background monitoring as well as stack monitoring to assess an organisation’s contribution to pollution, and the effect this has compared to natural levels

- Natural Gas Ultrasonic Flowmeter:

Ultrasonic Gas Meter for Custody Transfer and Process Gas Measurement

Natural gas flows through pipelines or into storage tanks, often changing hands along its route. This always means that the gas flow rate must be measured with the highest accuracy and precision. Tiny measuring errors can have a significant economic impact due to the large amounts of natural gas involved. A stable gas meter should remain unaffected by adverse measuring conditions, such as background noise in the pipeline. This challenge is effectively handled by ultrasonic gas meters.

These meters can be equipped with multiple non-reflective, chordal measuring paths, making them ideal for applications ranging from high-accuracy custody transfer to less precise requirements like flare and underground storage.

Custody Transfer Measurement in Natural Gas Distribution

Local authorities purchase natural gas and sell it to users such as power stations, primary industries,and other industrial consumers. Given the considerable volumes of gas involved, precise and, above all,long-term reliable input and output measurements are crucial. Conventional mechanical gas meters are difficult to monitor and require costly maintenance, making them less practical.

Ultrasonic gas meters, designed specifically for the natural gas distribution market, address these challenges. They offer an efficient, low-maintenance, and highly accurate alternative to traditional mechanical meters.
